## PART 1: GENERAL STRATEGY ADVICE

### ‚úÖ **RECOMMENDED: SELLING PUTS** (Best Strategy)

**Why this works:**
1. **Collect Premium:** You get paid upfront (premium comes TO you)
2. **Time Decay Works FOR You:** Options lose value daily, helping your position
3. **Lower Entry if Assigned:** If stock doesn't recover and you get assigned, you buy at a discount
4. **Profit if Stock Recovers:** Put expires worthless, you keep the full premium

**Strategy:**
- Sell cash-secured puts at strikes **5-10% below current price** ($325-$345 range)
- Target **2-3 weeks to expiration** (sweet spot for time decay)
- Only sell if you're comfortable owning AVGO at that price
- Premium collected is yours to keep regardless

**Example:**
- Stock: $361
- Sell $340 Put (6% below) for ~$5 premium = **$500 per contract**
- If stock stays above $340: Keep $500 profit
- If stock drops below $340: You buy 100 shares at $340 (effective cost: $335 after premium)

**Risk/Reward:**
- ‚úÖ Max Profit: Premium collected (guaranteed if stock stays above strike)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Max Risk: Strike price √ó 100 - premium (if stock crashes, you must buy)
- ‚úÖ Probability: 70-85% profit (if strike is 5-10% OTM)

## KEY CONSIDERATIONS

### 1. **Timing**
- If recovery happens in 3+ weeks: Selling puts is better
- If recovery happens in 1 week: Buying calls could be better
- Your 1-2 week timeline favors **selling puts** (more time for premium decay)

### 2. **Capital Requirements**
- Selling puts: Need $34,000+ cash (for cash-secured)
- Buying calls: Need $1,500-$3,000 (much less)
- Consider margin for puts (20-25% of strike)

### 3. **Risk Tolerance**
- Low risk: Sell OTM puts (lower premium, lower risk)
- Medium risk: Sell ATM puts or buy ITM calls
- High risk: Buy OTM calls (cheap but low probability)

### 4. **Implied Volatility**
- High IV (after selloff): Premiums are expensive (good for selling, bad for buying)
- Low IV: Premiums cheap (good for buying, bad for selling)
- Check IV percentile/rank before deciding
